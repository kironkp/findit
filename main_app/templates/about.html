About FindIt

A small, opinionated organizer for people who keep losing things in their own home.

Why I built this

I forget where I put things constantly — passport, drill bits, the good scissors, that one charger. I tried spreadsheets, sticky notes, photos in a folder. Nothing stuck because the act of logging was always more work than just buying another pair of scissors.

FindIt is built around the moment you actually need it: standing in a closet, holding something, with one hand free. Add an item in three fields. Move it with one tap. After a lookup, the app asks "Found it here?" — and silently fixes itself when the answer is no.

How it works

  • Things are the items you own. Each one belongs to a location.
  • Locations nest. A safe lives inside a closet inside a bedroom. The app shows the breadcrumb so you know exactly where to look.
  • Tags are a shared catalog — fragile, electronics, seasonal, valuable. Use them across all your stuff.
  • Move history is automatic. Every move logs a timestamp so you can look at the trail when something's wandered off.

About the builder

Hi — I built FindIt because I wanted a project I'd actually keep using after the demo. So I picked the boring problem I have most often: where did I put the thing.

The whole app is intentionally small. No notifications, no native app, no AI. Just the cleanest possible loop between "put something away" and "find it again later."
